# SebsWebs data-handling routine

This checklist supports the public privacy notice. It must reflect what SebsWebs actually does in practice.

## Account security

- Keep two-factor authentication enabled on the Gmail and GitHub accounts used for SebsWebs.
- Use unique passwords stored in a password manager.
- Do not store client passwords in ordinary email, documents or spreadsheets.
- Only access client accounts where the project requires it and the client has authorised access.

## Public quote form

- Use form submissions only to respond to enquiries and prepare quotes.
- Do not ask for payment-card details, passwords or sensitive personal information through the form.
- Keep the honeypot spam field enabled.
- Review FormSubmit's data-processing, retention, subprocessor and international-transfer terms before relying on it for higher-risk or client-customer data.
- Replace FormSubmit if suitable processor terms and safeguards cannot be confirmed.

## Retention review

Review stored information at least every six months.

- Delete unsuccessful enquiries within 12 months of the last meaningful contact.
- Keep project correspondence, approvals and files for no longer than six years after completion unless a legal or contractual reason requires longer.
- Keep invoices and payment records for the applicable legal accounting period.
- Delete temporary credentials after handover unless ongoing management has been agreed.
- Delete duplicate downloads and local copies when they are no longer needed.

## Client projects

Before launching a site that collects customer submissions or stores client customer data:

- Identify whether the client is the controller and SebsWebs is acting as a processor.
- Agree data-protection responsibilities in writing.
- Record the hosting provider and any subprocessors.
- Agree access controls, backups, retention, deletion and handover arrangements.
- Confirm who supplies the site's privacy notice and cookie controls.
- Collect only the information needed for the feature.

## Rights requests

When someone asks to access, correct, delete, restrict or object to use of their information:

1. Record the request date.
2. Confirm identity using proportionate information.
3. Search relevant email, project folders and business records.
4. Respond within the applicable legal deadline.
5. Record what was provided, corrected, retained or deleted and why.

## Suspected data breach

- Secure the affected account or system immediately.
- Record what happened, the information involved, affected people and actions taken.
- Change exposed credentials and remove unauthorised access.
- Assess whether the incident risks people's rights and freedoms.
- Seek appropriate legal or ICO guidance and notify the ICO or affected people where required.

## Change control

Update `privacy.html`, this checklist and any client agreement whenever analytics, marketing tools, new form providers, payment tools, customer accounts or other personal-data features are introduced.
